Understanding Token 6 Transactions

Token 6 transactions refer to specific events involving the sale, transfer, or exchange of a digital asset. These transactions include:

  • Token Sales: The sale of a cryptocurrency for fiat currency, such as USD or EUR, results in capital gains, which are taxable.
  • Token Exchanges: The swapping of one cryptocurrency for another, such as trading Bitcoin for Ethereum, is considered a capital gain or loss.
  • Token Transfers: Moving a digital asset from one wallet or exchange to another can result in capital gains, depending on the tax authority's jurisdiction.

To accurately calculate capital gains from Token 6 transactions, it is essential to track the following information:

  • Transaction dates and amounts: Record the date and value of each transaction to determine capital gains.
  • Cost basis: Calculate the cost basis of each token using the initial purchase price, fees, and any subsequent transfers.
  • Holding period: Determine the duration the token was held, as this affects the tax rate applied.
