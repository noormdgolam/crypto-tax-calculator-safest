Calculating Capital Gains and Losses

Capital gains are profits made from selling cryptocurrencies at a higher price than their original purchase price. Conversely, capital losses occur when the sale price is lower than the original purchase price. To calculate capital gains and losses, users can use the following formula:

Capital Gain/Loss = (Sales Amount - Cost Basis) x Number of Tokens Sold/Bought

The cost basis is the original purchase price of the token, and the sales amount is the price at which it was sold. The number of tokens sold/bought refers to the quantity of tokens involved in the transaction.

For example, if an individual bought 100 Token 4 at $10 per token and sold 50 at $20, their capital gain would be:

Capital Gain = ($20 - $12.50) x 50 = $625

Taxable Event Types

There are two main types of taxable events when it comes to Token 4 transactions: gains and losses. Gains arise when the sale price exceeds the original purchase price, while losses occur when the sale price is lower than the original purchase price. Both gains and losses are subject to taxation.

Here are the different types of taxable events and their corresponding tax implications:

  • Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG): Gains from selling cryptocurrencies within one year of their purchase are considered short-term capital gains and are subject to ordinary income tax rates.
  • Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG): Gains from selling cryptocurrencies held for more than one year are considered long-term capital gains and are subject to preferential tax rates.
  • Short-Term Capital Loss (STCL): Losses from selling cryptocurrencies within one year of their purchase are considered short-term capital losses and can be used to offset short-term capital gains.
  • Long-Term Capital Loss (LCL): Losses from selling cryptocurrencies held for more than one year are considered long-term capital losses and can be used to offset long-term capital gains.

It is essential to note that tax laws and regulations may vary depending on an individual's jurisdiction and the specific tax laws applicable to their location.
