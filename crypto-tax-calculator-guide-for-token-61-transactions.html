Understanding Token 61 Taxation

Most countries consider cryptocurrencies as property, and the tax treatment is similar to that of real estate. When selling Token 61 for fiat currency, the transaction generates a capital gain or loss.

  1. Long-term gain: A gain on sale of Token 61 that's held for more than one year qualifies as long-term capital gain and might be subject to a lower tax rate.
  2. Short-term gain: Conversely, a token held for less than one year is subject to ordinary tax rates.
  3. Wash sales: Traders who sell a token at a loss and buy it back soon after may find their short-term loss disallowed due to 'wash sales' rules.
  4. Netting transactions: Taxpayers can offset losses in one transaction against gains in another, reducing net tax liabilities.

In case of a loss:

  • A taxpayer may offset up to $3,000 in capital losses against ordinary income, but any excess loss can be carried forward for up to 20 years.
  • A loss from a token held in a margin account could be offset by gains from any other assets in that account.
