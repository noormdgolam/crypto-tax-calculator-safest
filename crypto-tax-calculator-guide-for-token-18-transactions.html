Understanding Crypto Taxation Basics

Cryptocurrency taxation falls under the purview of the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) and is governed by the same rules as traditional financial transactions. The tax implications of cryptocurrency purchases, sales, and exchanges can be complex and nuanced, making it essential to have a clear understanding of the tax calculation process.

For individuals, taxable events occur when they realize a gain or loss from the disposition of a digital asset. Gains are calculated by subtracting the cost basis (the price at which the asset was acquired) from the selling price. Losses are similarly calculated, but are typically only deductible to the extent of gains realized during the tax year.

For businesses, cryptocurrency transactions are subject to the same tax principles as other business income and expenses. Businesses that accept cryptocurrency as payment must report the value of the received cryptocurrency in US dollars using a recognized exchange rate. Similarly, businesses that hold cryptocurrencies as assets must calculate their value in US dollars at the end of each tax year.

Avoiding Common Tax Errors

Crypto tax errors can be costly and may lead to significant penalties. To avoid these errors, it's essential to accurately track and report all cryptocurrency transactions. Here are some common mistakes to watch out for:

  • Failing to report or underreporting gains
  • Misclassifying assets (e.g., treating a cryptocurrency sale as a non-taxable event)
  • Incorrectly calculating cost basis
  • Failing to account for wash sales and like-kind exchanges

By using the Token 18 tax calculator and following best tax practices, individuals and businesses can accurately report their cryptocurrency transactions and avoid costly mistakes.
